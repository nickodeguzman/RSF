Vision

Richwell Colleges is an institution transforming the lives of the youth towards success and prosperity through excellent education focused on competencies and skills anchored on the values of excellence, integrity, perseverance, love of parents, kindness for others, ingrained patriotism, care for environment, and faithful reverence in God.

Richwell Colleges Incorporated will be a University

Mission


I. Make competencies and excellence as the standard for everything the institution has to offer, and the norm for which the graduates will become.
II. Mold the students with the character and ability to seek the answers to many questions and unknowns that they will encounter in their journey to success.
III. Develop with the students the traits and dicipline of leadership and so they could reach out to become strategic and respected leaders way beyond just being simply educated.
IV. Motivate the students in entrepreneurial vision to harness the nation's rich resources, generate job opportunities, and make a difference for a prosperous country.
V. Nurture and strengthen the spiritual dimension and faithful reverence in God in the life of every student.
VI. Make the life of every student anchored on moral and spiritual values.

Core Values


Excellence, integrity, industry, perseverance, love of parents, kindness for others, ingrained patriotism, care for the environment.

Process


The Richwell Foundation scholarship fund shall be managed by an Administrator reporting to the board of Trustees. The administrator shall provide the monthly statement of acoounts to the benefactors.

An official receipt will be issued by Richwell Scholarship Foundation to the benefator for evey scholarship fund contribution.

The benefactor shall designate the scholarship beneficiaries and the amounts of grant, or shall assign such authority to the Foundation or to Richwell Colleges.

The scholarship beneficiaries will be guided bt the Foundation and Richwell COlleges to the principle of participation in the cost of college education with the appreciation that the scholarship grant is an assistance; not an entitlement.
